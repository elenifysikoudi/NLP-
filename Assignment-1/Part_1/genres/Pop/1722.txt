There's a spot in old Tir conaell there's a wee house in the glen Where dwelt an fairest colleen who charmed the hearts of men She was winsome hale and hearty shy and graceful as the dawn Neighbours loved that widow's daughter happy laughing Noreen Bawn Till one day there came a letter with her passage paid to go To the land where the Missouri and the Mississippi flows Then she said goodbye to Ireland and next morning at the door That old mother broken hearted bid farewell to Noreen Bawn Many years that mother waited till one evening at the door Stood a gorgeous looking lady costly were the clothes she wore Saying mother dont you know me for I've only got a cold But thoes purple spots upon her cheeks the tragic story's told
